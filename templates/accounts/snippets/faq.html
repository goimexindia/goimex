How does the fulfilment of orders work on Goimex.com?

    International sellers have two options to fulfil orders:

  1. - The seller fulfils and ships the orders directly to the buyer’s address, using a carrier of the seller’s choice. The Incoterms (International Commercial Terms) for the products listed need to be selected correctly for the buyer to know how the products will be delivered. The fulfilment lead time is set by the seller when listing the products in the Seller Centre.

    2. OR


  2. - Sellers can ship the inventory to Goimex. Goimex will store it and fulfil orders as they are placed and manage the inventory on a consignment basis. Goimex will act as an Importer of Record and certify the products if required by the local authorities. Storage and certification will be an additional cost to the seller, and sellers must sign a Consignment Agreement with Gooimex.

Who do I invoice when my products are sold?

    The seller will issue the invoice directly to the buyer unless Goimex is acting as the Merchant of Record on a particular transaction.

How do I get insurance for my own cargo and who should pay for it?

    This depends entirely on the Incoterms (International Commercial Terms) used in the transaction. For example, if CIF (Cost, Insurance and Freight) or CIP (Carriage and Insurance Paid) are used, it is the seller’s responsibility to pay for the insurance as well as the shipping fees. For more info about different Incoterms and their meaning, please contact us. Regardless of the Incoterms used, both the seller and the buyer can reach a mutual agreement on insurance.

Do I need to register or certify my goods and what is the process?

    Yes, most products are regulated. Goimex offers support with product registration and quality certifications of all types. Our logistics experts will guide you through the process. In joint venture we provide goods inspection and quality control. For more information, please contact our logistics team.

How to obtain accurate shipping rates on my orders or shipments?

How can I track my shipment?

If the transaction type is “buyer collects”, or if the seller uses Goimex logistics, either party will provide regular updates to the seller as well as provide a tracking number. This may be in the form of an Airway Number or Bill of Lading Number. Upon delivery, the seller will receive proof of delivery from the buyer or from the transporter. For more information, please contact our logistics team.

What are the customs fundamentals and how can I be compliant?

Custom clearance processes in the region - especially UAE - are highly digital and thorough. There are many government resources on this subject in different languages. All you need for clearance (if the cargo type is not regulated) are the following: a commercial invoice, packing list and airway bill (or seaway bill, depending on the shipping mode) and Certificate of Origin (COO) if the shipment is by sea. COO is not required when using air cargo. However, if the cargo type is regulated (meaning the items need further certification like in the case of Food & Beverage), a pre-certification is required from the authorities prior to shipping. To determine the nature of certification required, your transporter or Goimex Logistics Team may guide you through the process by checking the HS Codes (Harmonized Systems Codes) provided by the seller.

How do religious celeberations impact operations in the region?

Ramadan is a Holy month observed by Muslims worldwide as the month of fasting, prayer, piety, and is considered one of the five pillars in Islam. It lasts for about 29 days followed by 3 days of holidays. Every year Ramadan starts and concludes about 10 days earlier in the calendar, for example in 2022, it start on 13 April. During Ramadan, working hours in the public and private sectors are reduced to 6 hours a day usually from 09h00 till 14h00. Workload and capacity are somewhat reduced, therefore, international sellers might experience slower than usual responses and some delays. Sellers are advised to prepare for transactions in advance.

What happens if my shipment or packages get damaged?

You should always consider insurance when transporting goods. Whether you’re using Goimex logistics or a 3rd party provider, please make sure to ask about good insurance coverage and check all the inclusions. Nonetheless, taking precautions also greatly helps reduce the risk of damage like the following: Make sure the packages are packed in strong and durable materials and covered in shrink wrap films to help avoid moisture while in transit. Its recommended photograph the cargo before handing it over to the transporter. These images can be useful later to claim insurance in case of damage or loss. Choosing a reliable transporter helps mitigate any damage risk. If your transporter suggests proper packing, then you should consider this step.

Can I store the goods in a third party facility and distribute locally?

Yes, this can be highly effective financially speaking. There are legal requirements if a seller opts for bulk-shipping, storage and distribution within the country/region. Most importantly, the goods should enter the country and be cleared under a registered commercial license. The IOR (Importer of Record) becomes the “named” importer and distributor on behalf of the seller and Goimex may facilitate this transaction (importation, clearance, storage & last-mile delivery, including invoicing) by importing on behalf of the seller on a consignment basis. Please contact our logistics teams for more details.
